## Phase Goal

**As a** host embedding the PhantomStream viewer, **I want to** mirror a `<video>`/`<audio>` element's playback (play/pause/seek/rate) from the captured tab over a new throttled side channel with drift-corrected sync, **so that** the viewer plays the source media in lockstep without the bytes ever crossing the relay and without wedging on autoplay policy.

<objective>
Land the wire + logic spine for media-playback sync: the `STREAM.MEDIA` protocol op, the `MEDIA_SYNC_THROTTLE_MS` constant, the media typedefs, and — the core deliverable — the **pure** `reconcileMediaDrift` function with its full table-test suite. This slice is independently verifiable end-to-end in isolation (no DOM, no capture, no renderer): a consumer can decode a `STREAM.MEDIA` message and call the reconciler to get a drift action. Per RESEARCH this slice carries the bulk of the phase's automated value.

Purpose: The reconciler is the phase's novel contribution (tolerance band + rate-nudge + live-edge over rrweb's unconditional-seek baseline). Keeping it pure and zero-dependency in `src/protocol/` makes MWIRE-02 ("jsdom-unit-testable, no real media timeline") true by construction and lets both capture and renderer share it.
Output: `STREAM.MEDIA` op, `MEDIA_SYNC_THROTTLE_MS`, `MediaSyncPayload`/`MediaBaselineEntry` typedefs, `src/protocol/media-reconcile.js`, the reconciler table tests, and protocol-export/envelope-round-trip assertions.
</objective>
