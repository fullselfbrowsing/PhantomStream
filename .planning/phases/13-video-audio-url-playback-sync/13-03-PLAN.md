## Phase Goal

**As a** host embedding the PhantomStream viewer, **I want to** mirror a `<video>`/`<audio>` element's playback (play/pause/seek/rate) from the captured tab over a new throttled side channel with drift-corrected sync, **so that** the viewer plays the source media in lockstep without the bytes ever crossing the relay and without wedging on autoplay policy.

<objective>
The renderer slice: receive `STREAM.MEDIA`, run the pure reconciler, and drive the in-iframe media element from the parent realm — plus the autoplay-correct muted default, the `play()`-rejection affordance + `onMediaBlocked` callback, the muted→unmuted-source unmute affordance, the `media-src` CSP add, the string-layer media URL gate (the load-bearing SSRF fix), and the `mediaMode` poster/reference split. After this slice the viewer plays a mirrored video in lockstep and never wedges on a blocked play.

Purpose: The in-iframe element is inert (no `allow-scripts`); the parent realm is the only tier that can drive it cross-realm. This slice closes the viewer-side-fetch SSRF hole at the authoritative pre-parse string layer (the browser prefetches `<video src>`/`poster` during srcdoc parse — a post-parse scrub is too late) and makes the mirror autoplay-policy-correct.
Output: `media-src` in `CSP_META`, the generalized `gateSnapshotAssets` covering media tags, the three media affordance renderFns, `handleMedia` + the parent-realm driver + the unmute trigger + `mediaMode` gate, and two renderer test files.
</objective>
